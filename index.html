Welcome, adventurer, to a landmark in gaming history. Zork is one of the earliest interactive fiction games and a direct descendant of the original mainframe text adventure, Colossal Cave Adventure. Prepare to enter a world of sprawling dungeons, perplexing puzzles, and dangerous creatures. Your greatest tools will be your wits and your words.

About This Version

This is a faithful web-based port of the original 1977 version of Zork. Every effort has been made to preserve the classic experience, from the parser to the puzzles, so you can enjoy it on any modern browser.

The game you are about to play is more than just a game; it's an artifact from the golden age of mainframe computing.

This port was developed directly from the original MDL (MIT Design Language) source code written between 1977 and 1979 at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. The original creators were Tim Anderson, Marc Blank, Bruce Daniels, and Dave Lebling, members of the MIT Laboratory for Computer Science Dynamic Modelling Group.

The source code was preserved on magnetic tape and recovered as part of the MIT, Tapes of Tech Square (ToTS) collection. Originally created on a PDP-10 timesharing computer, these files represent a foundational piece of interactive entertainment.
